# AI Word Kill List — Full Reference

## Tier 1 — Instant Red Flags (Always Replace)
- delve → explore, look into, dig into, examine
- tapestry → mix, combination, blend, picture
- realm → field, area, space, world
- meticulous(ly) → careful, thorough, precise
- testament to → proof of, evidence that, shows that
- it is important to note → just say the thing directly
- in today's ever-evolving world → delete entirely or rewrite with specific context
- in the realm of → in [specific field], among [specific people]
- Certainly! → never use as opener
- Absolutely! → never use
- pivotal → key, central, critical, turning point
- multifaceted → complex, layered, multiple-sided

## Tier 2 — Common AI Vocabulary (Replace When Clustered)
Verbs: utilize, facilitate, commence, endeavour, underscore, harness, leverage, navigate (metaphorical), bolster, illuminate, embark, garner, showcase
Adjectives: cutting-edge, groundbreaking, revolutionary, transformative, vibrant, intricate, commendable, paramount, crucial, invaluable, comprehensive, robust, seamless(ly), impactful
Nouns: beacon, landscape (metaphorical), ecosystem (metaphorical), paradigm, synergy, thought leader, stakeholder, nuance (when used vaguely)

## Tier 3 — Structural AI Tells (Rewrite the Pattern)
Formulaic openers (never use):
- 'In today's fast-paced world...'
- 'In the ever-evolving landscape of...'
- 'As we navigate the complexities of...'
- 'In the age of [X], it has never been more important to...'
- 'Whether you're a [X] or a [Y]...'
- 'At its core, [topic] is about...'

Hollow closers (never use):
- 'In conclusion, ...'
- 'In summary, ...'
- 'In essence, ...'
- 'Ultimately, ...'
- 'To sum up, ...'
- 'As we have seen, ...'
- 'It is clear that ...'

Meta-commentary (delete entirely):
- 'In this article / essay / post, we will explore...'
- 'As mentioned earlier / above...'
- 'This section will cover...'
- 'Before we dive in...'
- 'Let's explore each of these in turn.'

AI structural clichés:
- 'Not just X, but Y'
- 'It isn't X, it's Y'
- 'This is where X comes in'
- 'X is more than just Y; it's Z'
- 'The answer lies in...'
- Listing exactly three points in every argument
- Ending every section with a transition sentence to the next

## Tier 4 — Punctuation Red Flags
- Em dash overuse: max 1 per 300 words
- Semicolons: use full stops instead when in doubt
- Excessive bold/italics for every key phrase

## Model-Specific Fingerprints
ChatGPT (GPT-4o): Opens with 'Certainly!' or 'Absolutely!', high em dash density, 'delve/tapestry/realm/testament to', balanced-argument structure on every topic
Claude (Anthropic): Opens with 'I'd be happy to...' or 'I'd love to...', longer hedged sentences, heavy em dash and comma-clause use
Gemini: More conversational but over-structured, bullet-heavy, 'Let's explore', 'Dive into'
GPT-5+: Fewer em dashes, more semicolons, still over-formal in academic contexts

## Words That Look Like AI But Aren't Always
Context-dependent — don't blindly replace: keen (common British/Australian), crucial (legitimate in urgent contexts), insights (used in business by humans too), furthermore (acceptable once per document)
