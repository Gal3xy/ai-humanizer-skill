# AI Word Kill List — Full Reference

## Tier 1 — Instant Red Flags (Always Replace)

These appear in AI output at a rate that makes them immediately recognizable. No exceptions.

- delve → explore, look into, dig into, examine
- tapestry → mix, combination, blend, picture
- realm → field, area, space, world
- meticulous(ly) → careful, thorough, precise
- testament to → proof of, evidence that, shows that
- it is important to note → just say the thing directly
- in today's ever-evolving world → delete entirely or rewrite with specific context
- in the realm of → in [specific field], among [specific people]
- Certainly! → never use as opener
- Absolutely! → never use
- pivotal → key, central, critical, turning point
- multifaceted → complex, layered, multiple-sided
- tapestry → mix, picture, blend
- nestled → sitting, located, set in
- foster → build, develop, grow
- bolster → strengthen, support, boost
- illuminate → show, reveal, make clear
- showcase → show, display, demonstrate
- garner → get, earn, draw
- underscore → highlight, show, point to
- navigate (metaphorical) → handle, deal with, work through

## Tier 2 — Common AI Vocabulary (Replace When Clustered)

Two or more of these in the same paragraph is a flag.

**Verbs:** utilize, facilitate, commence, endeavour, harness, leverage, embark, bolster, illuminate, garner, showcase, foster, underscore, navigate (metaphorical)

**Adjectives:** cutting-edge, groundbreaking, revolutionary, transformative, vibrant, intricate, commendable, paramount, crucial, invaluable, comprehensive, robust, seamless(ly), impactful, pivotal, multifaceted, meticulous, breathtaking, renowned

**Nouns:** beacon, landscape (metaphorical), ecosystem (metaphorical), paradigm, synergy, thought leader, stakeholder, nuance (when used vaguely), tapestry, realm, testament

## Tier 3 — Structural AI Tells (Rewrite the Pattern)

These are harder to spot because they're not single words — they're sentence or paragraph patterns.

**Formulaic openers (never use):**
- "In today's fast-paced world..."
- "In the ever-evolving landscape of..."
- "As we navigate the complexities of..."
- "In the age of [X], it has never been more important to..."
- "Whether you're a [X] or a [Y]..."
- "At its core, [topic] is about..."

**Hollow closers (never use):**
- "In conclusion, ..."
- "In summary, ..."
- "In essence, ..."
- "Ultimately, ..."
- "To sum up, ..."
- "As we have seen, ..."
- "It is clear that ..."
- "The future looks bright..."
- "Exciting times lie ahead..."

**Meta-commentary (delete entirely):**
- "In this article / essay / post, we will explore..."
- "As mentioned earlier / above..."
- "This section will cover..."
- "Before we dive in..."
- "Let's explore each of these in turn."

**Chatbot artifacts (delete entirely):**
- "I hope this helps"
- "Would you like me to expand on any section?"
- "Feel free to ask if you have questions"
- "Of course!"
- "Great question!"
- "You're absolutely right!"
- "As an AI language model..."

**Knowledge-cutoff hedges (delete or rewrite):**
- "as of my last update"
- "while specific details are limited"
- "based on my training data"

## Tier 4 — AI Structural Clichés (Rewrite the Construction)

**Negative parallelisms (use max once; replace the rest):**
- "Not just X, but Y"
- "It isn't X, it's Y"
- "Not only X, but also Y"
- "Not just X, but Y and Z"

**Forced structure:**
- Listing exactly three points for every argument (rule of three overuse)
- Ending every section with a sentence that transitions to the next
- "This is where X comes in"
- "X is more than just Y; it's Z"
- "The answer lies in..."
- "This is where things get interesting"

**False ranges (usually meaningless; delete or make specific):**
- "from startups to Fortune 500 companies"
- "from beginners to experts"
- "from small towns to major cities"
- "from X to Y" where X and Y aren't on a meaningful scale

**Balanced-argument hedging (pick a side):**
- "On one hand... on the other hand..."
- "While X has benefits, it also has drawbacks..."
- "It depends on the situation..."

## Tier 5 — Copula Avoidance (Subtle but Detectable)

AI replaces simple "is/are/has" with verbs that sound more elevated. Revert them.

- "serves as" → is
- "stands as" → is
- "marks" (when meaning "is") → is / represents
- "boasts" → has
- "features" (when meaning "has") → has
- "represents" (when meaning "is") → is
- "acts as" → is / works as
- "functions as" → is / works as

## Tier 6 — Punctuation Red Flags

- Em dash overuse: max 1 per 300 words (Claude fingerprint when higher)
- Semicolons: use full stops instead when in doubt
- Excessive bold/italics for every key phrase (inline-header bullet format is a major tell)
- Curly quotes in contexts where straight quotes feel more natural

## Tier 7 — Synonym Cycling

AI avoids repeating words by substituting synonyms. This creates an unnatural "elegant variation" that human writers don't do. If the text rotates through "significant / notable / considerable / meaningful / substantial" for the same concept, that's AI.

Fix: pick one word and use it. Repetition is human.

## Model-Specific Words to Watch For

**ChatGPT (GPT-4o):** delve, tapestry, realm, testament to, pivotal, multifaceted, meticulous, certainly, absolutely, "not just... but"

**Claude (Anthropic):** I'd be happy to, I'd love to, certainly, nuanced, comprehensive, straightforward, robust, dive into (as metaphor), em dashes used heavily

**Gemini:** Let's explore, dive into, vibrant, showcasing, highlighting, fostering, bullet-heavy structure

**GPT-5+:** Fewer em dashes, more semicolons, synonym cycling is pronounced, still over-formal in academic registers

## Context-Dependent Words (Don't Blindly Replace)

These can appear in genuine human writing depending on context:
- keen — common in British/Australian writing
- crucial — legitimate in urgent contexts
- insights — used in business by real humans too
- furthermore — acceptable once per document
- indeed — acceptable in formal British writing
- innovative — overused but not always AI
